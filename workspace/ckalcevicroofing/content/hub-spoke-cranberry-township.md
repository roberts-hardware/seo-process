## Common Roofing Issues in Cranberry Township

### Ice Dams (Winter)

Cranberry Township's cold winters create perfect conditions for ice dams:

**What causes them:**
- Heat escaping through inadequate attic insulation
- Poor attic ventilation
- Temperature fluctuations

**Our solutions:**
- Proper attic ventilation (ridge vents, soffit vents)
- Adequate insulation (R-49 or higher)
- Ice and water shield at eaves
- Emergency ice dam removal services

### Storm Damage (Year-Round)

Severe weather is common in Butler County:

**Wind damage:** High winds lift shingles, damage flashing, tear off entire sections
**Hail damage:** Impact damage invisible from ground but allows water penetration
**Tree damage:** Falling limbs or entire trees puncture roofs

**Our response:** 24/7 emergency service with rapid dispatch and immediate temporary repairs.

### Aging Shingle Roofs

Most Cranberry Township homes have asphalt shingle roofs with 20-25 year lifespans:

**Signs your roof is aging:**
- Curling or cupping shingles
- Missing granules (shingles look "bald")
- Cracked or broken shingles
- Daylight visible through roof boards
- Increased energy bills (poor insulation)

**Our recommendation:** Schedule a free inspection every 5 years after the 15-year mark. We'll tell you honestly whether you need repair, maintenance, or replacement.

## Cranberry Township Roofing FAQs

**How long does a roof replacement take in Cranberry Township?**

Most residential roof replacements take 1-3 days:

- Day 2: Complete installation, flashing, cleanup
- Day 3 (if needed): Final touches, inspection, debris removal

We work efficiently while maintaining quality. Larger homes or complex roofs may take longer. Weather can impact timelines.

**Do I need a permit for roofing work in Cranberry Township?**

Yes. Cranberry Township requires building permits for roof replacements and most major repairs.

**Good news:** We handle all permit applications and inspections. You don't need to deal with paperwork or township offices—we manage the entire process.

**What roofing materials are best for Cranberry Township?**

Top choices for Cranberry Township homes:

1. **Architectural shingles:** Best value, 25-30 year lifespan, excellent appearance
2. **Impact-resistant shingles:** Hail protection, insurance discounts available
3. **Metal roofing:** Premium option, 40-50+ years, energy efficient
4. **Designer shingles:** High-end appearance, 30-50 year warranties

We'll recommend the best option for your budget, home style, and long-term goals.

**Will insurance cover my roof repair or replacement?**

Depends on the damage cause:

**Usually covered:**
- Storm damage (wind, hail, lightning)
- Fallen trees
- Fire
- Vandalism

**Not usually covered:**
- Normal wear and tear
- Deferred maintenance
- Gradual deterioration
- Cosmetic issues

We provide detailed documentation to support legitimate insurance claims and work directly with adjusters.

**How much does a new roof cost in Cranberry Township?**

Typical Cranberry Township roof replacement costs:

- **Small home (1,200-1,500 sq ft):** $6,000-$10,000
- **Medium home (1,800-2,200 sq ft):** $10,000-$16,000
- **Large home (2,500-3,500 sq ft):** $16,000-$25,000

Factors affecting cost:
- Roof size and complexity
- Pitch/slope (steeper = more expensive)
- Material choice
- Number of layers to remove
- Flashing and ventilation upgrades needed

We provide detailed, itemized estimates—no surprises.
